THE ARTIST

M.I.A.

Understanding the artist behind Borders is essential to grasp the depth and authenticity of its message.

BIOGRAPHY

Who Is M.I.A.?

M.I.A., whose real name is Mathangi Arulpragasam, is a British musician, songwriter, producer and visual artist. She was born in Hounslow, London, in 1975, the daughter of a Tamil Sri Lankan father.

Her multicultural upbringing - growing up between Sri Lanka and the United Kingdom, amid a devastating civil war - profoundly influenced her music, which combines different styles with intense social and political themes.

She is widely recognized for songs like Paper Planes (2007), which became a global hit, and for her critically acclaimed album Kala (2007).

PERSONAL HISTORY

Connection With Migration

When M.I.A. was just six months old, her family moved to Sri Lanka, where she grew up in Jaffna - a region at the heart of the civil conflict between the government and the Tamil Tigers.

Her father, Arul Pragasam, was involved in the Tamil separatist movement. Her family lived under constant threat of violence and eventually had to relocate multiple times before gaining asylum in the United Kingdom.

This direct experience with forced displacement, refugee camps and the search for safety gave M.I.A. a unique and authentic perspective on the themes she explores in Borders.

ARTISTIC STYLE

Music With A Message

Unlike many mainstream artists, M.I.A. frequently uses her platform to discuss global issues such as war, poverty, inequality, migration and human rights - even when doing so generated controversy.

Her musical style is a fusion of hip-hop, electronic, funk and global influences, reflecting her own multicultural identity. She is also recognized for her highly conceptual art direction and music videos.

Her songs encourage listeners to think critically about society and to question structures of power that are frequently made invisible.

BORDERS

Why She Wrote The Song

Borders was created and released in 2015, during the peak of one of the largest refugee crises since World War II. That year, over one million people crossed the Mediterranean Sea toward Europe, many fleeing the conflict in Syria.

M.I.A. wanted to use her position as an artist to draw attention to the difficulties faced by people forced to leave their homes because of war, persecution and instability - experiences she herself knew closely.

Through the song and its music video, she challenges audiences to reflect on migration policies and the human impact of borders in a language that is both accessible and impactful.

Art can start conversations that politics often avoids.

M.I.A. uses her music as a tool of social critique - transforming personal experiences of displacement into a universal message about human dignity.
