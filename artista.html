THE ARTIST

M.I.A.

Understanding the artist behind Borders helps us understand the powerful message of the song.

BIOGRAPHY

Who Is M.I.A.?

M.I.A., whose real name is Mathangi Arulpragasam, is a British musician, songwriter and visual artist. She was born in London and spent part of her childhood in Sri Lanka before returning to the United Kingdom.

Her multicultural background strongly influenced her music, which often combines different musical styles with social and political themes.

PERSONAL HISTORY

Connection With Migration

M.I.A.'s family experienced displacement due to conflict in Sri Lanka. Because of this, themes such as migration, identity and belonging became important parts of her work.

Her personal experiences gave her a unique perspective on the challenges faced by immigrants and refugees around the world.

ARTISTIC STYLE

Music With A Message

Unlike many mainstream artists, M.I.A. frequently uses her music to discuss global issues such as war, poverty, inequality, migration and human rights.

Her songs encourage listeners to think critically about society and question structures of power.

BORDERS

Why She Wrote The Song

Borders was created during a period when the world was witnessing major refugee crises. M.I.A. wanted to draw attention to the difficulties faced by people who are forced to leave their homes because of war, persecution and instability.

Through the song and its music video, she challenges audiences to reflect on migration policies and the human impact of borders.

Art can start conversations that politics often avoids.
