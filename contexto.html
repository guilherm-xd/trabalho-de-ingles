REFUGEES

Why people leave home

A refugee does not leave by choice; they leave by necessity.

  • War, persecution and violence force people out.
  • The main difference is the lack of a real choice.
  • The journey is often long, expensive and dangerous.
2015

The refugee crisis

Borders was released when the crisis was dominating the news.

  • More than 1 million people crossed the Mediterranean in 2015.
  • Syria, Afghanistan and Iraq were among the main crises.
  • M.I.A. answered that moment with music and image.
CHALLENGES

What happens after arrival

Arriving safely does not mean being accepted.

  • Migrants face language barriers, bureaucracy and discrimination.
  • Many continue to be seen as outsiders.
  • Borders asks us to see people, not numbers.
MUSIC VIDEO

The visual message

The video shows crowds to stress the collective size of the crisis.

  • Boats, fences and exhausted bodies appear throughout the video.
  • The focus is not one person; it is a global phenomenon.
  • The imagery makes the audience face what is often ignored.
TODAY

Why it still matters

Sudan, Venezuela and climate displacement show the song's question is still alive.

  • Conflicts and economic crises still force displacement.
  • Environmental disasters also push families out of their homes.
  • Governments still debate borders above rights.

Migration

A global phenomenon, not an isolated problem.

Rights

Asylum is legal protection, not a favor.

Identity

Without belonging, rights become fragile.

Equality

The world does not offer the same mobility to everyone.

CONCLUSION

The message of Borders

Borders uses music to ask who deserves safety, mobility and dignity.

That is why the song still matters far beyond 2015.
