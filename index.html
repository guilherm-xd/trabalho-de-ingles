INTRODUCTION

Why Borders Matters

The Song

Released in 2015, Borders became one of the most discussed songs by M.I.A. because it directly addresses migration, refugees and the barriers people face when searching for safety and opportunity.

The Question

Who has the freedom to move around the world? Who is welcomed and who is rejected? These questions sit at the center of the song - repeated with growing urgency.

The Message

Borders challenges the audience to think about privilege, nationality, identity and the human consequences of global inequality, connecting art and activism.

MAIN IDEA

More Than A Song

Borders is not only a musical work. It is a social commentary that encourages people to examine how borders affect human lives and how societies respond to refugees and immigrants.

Through provocative lyrics and powerful visual storytelling, M.I.A. transforms music into a discussion about freedom, dignity and human rights - themes that remain urgent today.

"What's up with that?"

The repeated question throughout the song invites listeners to challenge every system of control, every barrier and every inequality that defines who is free to move around the world.
