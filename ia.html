RESEARCH PROCESS

How We Used AI

A critical and honest reflection on the role of Artificial Intelligence in our research process - what worked, what failed and what we learned.

OVERVIEW

Our Journey With AI

To build this project, we used Artificial Intelligence tools - especially Claude (by Anthropic) - as a research and content production assistant. Three of our group's seven members work with systems development, which allowed us to use AI in a more in-depth way: from building the website to verifying data and organizing content.

This page answers the five guiding questions about AI use, with real examples of the prompts we used and a critical analysis of what worked - and what did not.

HOW WE USED IT

Process Steps

1

Initial research on M.I.A. and the song

We used AI to get an initial overview of the artist, her life story and the context behind creating Borders. We then verified the most important information in external sources such as Wikipedia, news sites and published interviews.

2

Thematic analysis of the lyrics

We asked AI to help identify and explain the main themes in the song - freedom of movement, identity, privilege, human rights. We compared the generated analysis with academic sources and music criticism articles to verify accuracy.

3

Research on refugee data

For the Context and News pages, we asked AI to point to the main data sources on migration and forced displacement. We then went directly to the UNHCR, IOM and other websites to confirm the numbers - and corrected some that AI had reported incorrectly.

4

Website construction

We used AI to help structure and write the HTML and CSS code for the website, ensuring visual and technical consistency across all pages. The final design went through manual review and adjustments by the team.

5

Translation and bilingualism

To create the English-Portuguese toggle feature, we used AI to translate the content and suggest the technical structure of the translation system in JavaScript. We manually reviewed all translations to ensure naturalness and accuracy.

REAL EXAMPLES

Prompts We Used

PROMPT 1
"Explain M.I.A.'s song Borders: what is the main message, what themes does it address and why was it released in 2015? Give me historical context."
✓ Worked well - generated a useful initial overview. We verified dates and historical facts in external sources before using.
PROMPT 2
"Analyze theme by theme the lyrics of Borders by M.I.A. What are the main symbols of the song? What did M.I.A. mean by repeating 'What's up with that?'"
✓ Generated a detailed and coherent analysis. We compared with music criticism articles to validate the interpretations.
PROMPT 3
"What are the most recent UNHCR data on the number of refugees in the world? Include data from 2024 and 2025."
⚠ AI made an error here - it reported outdated 2023 numbers. We went to the official UNHCR website (unhcr.org) and found the correct 2025 data.
PROMPT 4
"Create a complete HTML and CSS website for a school project about M.I.A.'s song Borders, with dark/light mode, language toggle (English/Portuguese) and multiple pages."
✓ Worked very well for the technical structure. We made manual adjustments to the design, content and mobile responsiveness.
PROMPT 5
"Connect the themes of the song Borders with recent news and events from 2024 and 2025 about migration and refugees."
✓ Generated good thematic connections. We verified all mentioned facts and data in sources such as UNHCR, IOM before publishing.
CRITICAL REFLECTION

The Five Questions About AI

1
PROMPTS

What prompts/questions did you use?

We used detailed and specific prompts - always including context (song name, artist, project goal) and asking for sources or pointers to verify. The more specific the prompt, the better the response. We learned that "explain Borders by M.I.A. in the context of the 2015 refugee crisis" produces much better results than just "talk about the song Borders."

2
ERRORS

Did AI make any mistakes?

Yes - and this was one of the most important discoveries of our process. The AI presented outdated data on the number of refugees (from the 2023 report, not 2025). Also, in an initial prompt, it confused some dates in M.I.A.'s biography. These errors reinforced the importance of never blindly trusting AI responses, especially when they involve numerical data and dates.

3
VERIFICATION

How did you verify the information?

We adopted a rule: every factual piece of information (dates, numbers, names, events) needed to be confirmed in at least one external source before going on the site. We consulted the official UNHCR website (unhcr.org), IOM reports (iom.int), press articles (The Guardian, Reuters, BBC) and Wikipedia. Whenever AI and the primary source diverged, we used the primary source's data.

4
CHALLENGES

What was easy and difficult about using AI?

Easy: structuring ideas, organizing content, writing code, suggesting connections between themes, translating texts. Difficult: obtaining up-to-date and precise data (AI has a knowledge cutoff), ensuring literary analyses were accurate and not too generic, and preventing responses from being excessively superficial. AI tends to sound confident even when it is wrong - which requires constant critical attention.

5
LEARNING

Did AI improve your understanding of the topic?

Yes - significantly. AI helped us identify connections between the song and historical context that we might not have seen alone, such as the relationship between the repetitive structure of the lyrics and the literary technique of anaphora. It also pointed us to sources we did not know. However, real understanding came when we went beyond AI: we read the reports, watched the music video carefully and discussed the themes as a group.

+
LIMITATIONS

Limitations We Discovered

AI does not replace critical thinking - it accelerates it. It does not know what is true, only what seems likely based on its training data. It can reproduce biases present in that data. It has no access to very recent events. And it tends to give answers that seem complete even when they are superficial. Using AI well requires knowing when to question its responses.

AI is a powerful tool - but critical thinking remains ours.

We used AI to work faster and better. But every piece of information was verified, every analysis was discussed and every creative decision was made by us. AI assisted our process - it did not replace it.
