LYRICS

Lyrics

Short speaking prompts about the lyrics, repetition and symbolism.

CORE MESSAGE

What the song says

The song asks why freedom of movement is not equal for everyone.

  • Borders is about migration, identity and inequality.
  • The language is simple, but the criticism is strong.
  • The repeated question forces the audience to think.
STRUCTURE

How the lyrics work

Each repetition adds more weight to the social critique.

  • M.I.A. lists systems: borders, politics, papers, passports.
  • Then she repeats the same question: "What's up with that?"
  • The effect is growing indignation.

TECHNIQUE

The power of repetition

Anaphora turns repetition into rhythm and pressure.

  • The song does not explain everything: it asks.
  • That repetition increases tension in each line.
  • That is why the critique becomes memorable.
SYMBOLISM

What a border represents

Here, a border is not just a fence: it is law, bureaucracy and exclusion.

  • Physical border: who enters and who stays out.
  • Social border: who belongs and who is treated as an outsider.
  • Political border: who receives protection and who does not.
